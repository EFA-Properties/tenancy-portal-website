Key Registrations & Resources

What landlords need
to register for

Direct links to the government bodies and resources every private landlord in England needs to be aware of.

DEADLINE 31 MAY 2026
Renters' Rights Act Information Sheet
Must be given to every existing tenant by 31 May 2026. Fines of up to £7,000 for non-compliance. Must be downloaded from gov.uk — other copies are not valid.
Download from gov.uk →
LATE 2026
Private Rented Sector Database
Mandatory registration for all private landlords in England. Requires contact details, property info, and proof of Gas Safety, EICR and EPC compliance.
Learn more on gov.uk →
MANDATORY 2028
PRS Landlord Ombudsman
All private landlords must join the new government-backed ombudsman scheme. Provides tenants with free, impartial dispute resolution. Legal requirement from 2028.
Learn more on gov.uk →
ONGOING REQUIREMENT
How to Rent Guide
Must be given to every new tenant at the start of their tenancy. Always use the current edition — older versions are not legally compliant.
Download latest edition →
RECOMMENDED
NRLA — National Residential Landlords Association
The UK's largest landlord association. Free advice line, legal guidance, and training. Essential for keeping up with the pace of legislative change.
Visit nrla.org.uk →
HMO LANDLORDS
HMO Licence — Local Council
Required if you rent to 5+ people from more than one household. Some councils require licences for smaller HMOs. Must be renewed every 5 years.
Check requirements on gov.uk →
LEGAL REQUIREMENT
Right to Rent Check
Landlords and agents must check every tenant has the legal right to rent in England before a tenancy starts. Fines of up to £20,000 per tenant for non-compliance. Use the official gov.uk online check service.
Run a check on gov.uk →
BEST PRACTICE
Inventory & Schedule of Condition
A detailed check-in inventory protects landlords in deposit disputes. Under the Renters' Rights Act, the burden of proof sits with the landlord — a signed, timestamped inventory is your strongest defence.
Guidance from DPS →
